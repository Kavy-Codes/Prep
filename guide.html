💎 The "Topper's Toolkit": High-Yield Content

1. The "Box Item" Trap

In school exams, teachers often pick questions from the "Boxes" (e.g., The Illusion of the Müller-Lyer or The Case of Phineas Gage). Do not skip these. ### 2. The 6-Mark "Flowchart" Strategy

To get 6/6, you must visualize.

  • For Memory: Draw the Atkinson-Shiffrin Model boxes.
  • For Motivation: Draw Maslow’s Pyramid.
  • For Development: Draw Bronfenbrenner’s Concentric Circles.
  • For Learning: Draw a table comparing Classical vs. Operant Conditioning.

3. Keywords You Must Memorize (Marks depend on these)

  • Reliability: Consistency of test scores.
  • Validity: Does the test measure what it claims to measure?
  • Homeostasis: The body's tendency to maintain a steady internal state (Motivation).
  • Schema: Mental framework for organizing information (Thinking/Development).
  • Chunking: Combining small bits of info into larger, meaningful units (Memory).

🧠 Active Recall Drill (The "Topper's" Secret)

Don't just read. After every chapter, close the book and answer these:

  1. Can I explain Classical Conditioning to a 5-year-old?
  2. Can I list the 4 stages of Piaget without looking?
  3. Can I name the 3 types of Memory and their duration?
